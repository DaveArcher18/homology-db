# Homology DB 0.0.1 observable contract

Status: release-gate recommendation for the Wayfinder ticket **Pin the v0.0.1 observable contract**.

## Decision

Homology DB `0.0.1` is useful rather than a toy only if a clean checkout can build an immutable, provenance-rich snapshot containing a nontrivial corpus of reconstructible finite models; serve exact structured queries over that snapshot; explain every match and every unknown; recompute the counted models through project-owned homology semantics; and reproduce the release's tests, benchmarks, and export hashes.

The hard gates below are the contract. A gate passes only from recorded evidence produced by the release candidate. Counts in a planning document, hand inspection of a few pages, or a green unit-test subset are not substitutes. These gates sharpen the map's destination—many reconstructible models, exact and partial torsion-pattern queries, and LLM-suitable evidence bundles—without expanding it to the UI or spectral-sequence work explicitly excluded there. [Wayfinder destination](../../.scratch/homology-db-v0-0-1/map.md#destination)

`MUST` denotes a `0.0.1` release gate. “Stretch” work is useful later but cannot compensate for a failed MUST.

## Hard release gates at a glance

| Gate | Minimum observable evidence |
|---|---|
| **G1 — Curated spaces** | At least **100** reviewed conceptual spaces, spanning at least **12** documented families/constructions; no one family supplies more than **25%** of the 100. |
| **G2 — Model corpus** | At least **1,000** distinct, reconstructible finite models: at least **400** in a versioned finite-simplicial format and at least **400** in a versioned finite-CW/regular-CW format. At least **25** curated spaces have two nonidentical models and at least **10** have models in both format classes. |
| **G3 — Torsion coverage** | At least **100 distinct `(curated space, positive degree)` pairs** have nonzero integral torsion, drawn from at least **40** curated spaces: at least 40 pairs with 2-primary, 25 with 3-primary, 15 with 5-primary, and 10 with 7-primary torsion. At least 8 spaces contain a cyclic summand of order `p^e` with `e >= 2`; at least 5 have primary-summand multiplicity at least 2; at least 10 torsion pairs occur outside degree 1. Categories may overlap. |
| **G4 — Mathematical grid** | Every curated space has a complete exact unreduced grid over `Z`, `F_2`, `F_3`, `F_5`, and `F_7` from degree 0 through its declared finite dimension, plus explicit reduced degree-zero values; at least 25 have a full reduced grid for the same coefficients/degrees. Every counted model has a complete exact unreduced integral grid. |
| **G5 — Adversarial coverage** | Outside the mandatory exact grid, the published data and contract fixtures contain at least **10** explicit unknown assertions, **10** not-computed assertions, **3** not-applicable assertions, **5** bounded/conjectural assertions, **5** correction chains, and **3** unresolved conflict sets, plus an empty model, a disconnected model, and exact-zero/reduced/unreduced degree-zero cases. |
| **G6 — Query contract** | All behaviours Q1–Q12 below pass through the public read interface against both a golden corpus and the released snapshot, with deterministic ordering and snapshot semantics. |
| **G7 — LLM envelope** | Every example-query response validates against the versioned envelope below and carries the normalized query, snapshot, coverage statement, match explanation, knowledge states, assertion IDs, and evidence references. |
| **G8 — Provenance/corrections** | Every counted model, relationship, and homology assertion is evidence-bearing; correction is append-only; old snapshot answers remain reproducible; absence is never interpreted as zero. |
| **G9 — Performance and bulk access** | The benchmark tier and p95/throughput limits below pass on the documented local reference host and produce a machine-readable report plus captured query plans. |
| **G10 — Owned computation** | Every model counted by G2 is parsed, converted to an ordered based chain complex, checked for `d^2 = 0`, and has integral homology recomputed by the owned high-level pipeline. Independent systems may verify results but are not canonical runtime objects or direct database writers. |
| **G11 — Reproducible release** | A clean checkout at tag `v0.0.1` reproduces the database/snapshot, tests, corpus audit, benchmark report, deterministic exports, manifest, changelog, and test log using documented commands. |

The 100/1,000 thresholds deliberately exceed the earlier 30–50-space, 50–100-model pilot while retaining its emphasis on structural coverage over inflated row count. [Project brief: pilot corpus](../planning/00-project-brief.md#pilot-corpus) A parameter sweep is not enough: the family cap, cross-format models, torsion strata, mathematical grid, and adversarial cases are what make the corpus useful for example retrieval.

## G1–G5: honest corpus semantics

### What counts as a curated space

A conceptual space counts toward G1 only when it has:

1. an immutable public ID, preferred name, and lifecycle state `reviewed`;
2. at least one citable reference or reviewed model assertion establishing what the space is;
3. a declared scope/completeness statement for its published integral homology;
4. at least one released model connected by an evidence-bearing `model_of` assertion; and
5. selected exact unreduced integral homology for every degree from 0 through the declared dimension, or an explicit documented reason that a finite degree bound—not a dimension—defines completeness.

Conceptual spaces and concrete presentations are different entities, and promotion from model results requires reviewed evidence; merging them would make provenance and duplicate handling dishonest. [Domain model: central distinction](../planning/01-domain-model.md#the-central-distinction) The two-tier corpus remains provisional in architecture but is observable here: curated spaces are the default example surface, while unidentified models are available only when the query explicitly selects the model corpus. [Working agreements: two-tier corpus](../planning/06-working-agreements.md#accepted-provisional-policy)

### What counts as a model

A model counts toward G2 only when all of the following pass automatically:

- its original bytes and normalized form have cryptographic hashes;
- its representation type and format version name a checked-in schema/decoder;
- a clean build can load it without an engine-native pickle/workspace;
- encode → decode → encode is deterministic under the documented normalization;
- the model has finite, explicitly ordered cells/simplices and enough attaching/face data to reconstruct the supported topological model—not merely its Betti numbers or an unlabelled boundary-matrix summary;
- conversion produces an ordered based chain complex whose adjacent differentials compose to zero;
- source, upstream version/retrieval identifier, importer version, and attribution are present; and
- duplicate artifacts remain separately attributable even if an asserted relationship later identifies their models.

The domain plan requires original and canonical hashes and forbids silently merging byte-distinct models. [Domain model: Model](../planning/01-domain-model.md#model) The source qualification checklist likewise requires exact upstream versions, stable identifiers, conventions, completeness, credits, and original checksums before import. [Candidate data-source landscape: qualification checklist](data-source-landscape.md#source-qualification-checklist)

The same mathematical simplicial complex may also define a regular CW complex, but one artifact counts in only one G2 format bucket. This prevents relabelling 400 simplicial records as 400 additional CW records. A chain complex alone is a computation artifact, not a finite-space model, and never counts toward G2.

### Torsion coverage audit

G3 is computed from selected, exact, unreduced integral homology assertions on curated conceptual spaces. Each `(public ID, degree)` counts at most once toward the 100-pair floor no matter how many primes or models establish the result. Prime subcategories may overlap. The audit MUST publish the qualifying public IDs, degrees, primes, exponents, and multiplicities for every threshold, so the counts can be reviewed rather than trusted as aggregate metadata.

For this audit:

- “has `p`-primary torsion” means the exact group has a nonzero primary cyclic summand at prime `p`;
- “cyclic summand of order `p^e`” means a direct summand `Z/(p^e)`, not merely a torsion subgroup whose total order is divisible by `p^e`;
- “multiplicity at least 2” means at least two copies of the same `Z/(p^e)` primary summand; and
- the degree requirement uses homological degree, not model dimension.

These distinctions follow the planned dual encoding: invariant factors for interchange and primary rows `(prime, exponent, multiplicity)` for indexed queries. [First tasks: mathematical encodings](../planning/03-first-tasks.md#task-2-freeze-the-mvp-mathematical-encodings)

### Mathematical-grid audit

G4 is a completeness promise over a finite, auditable rectangle, not a claim about all homology. For each curated space, the release manifest MUST give a declared finite dimension supported by a counted model, then enumerate selected exact groups in every degree `0..dimension` for `Z`, `F_2`, `F_3`, `F_5`, and `F_7`. It MUST also state that groups above that model dimension vanish under the model assertion being used. Reduced degree zero is explicit for every curated space; at least 25 spaces publish the complete reduced grid. Every G2-counted model has the analogous exact unreduced integral grid. A matrix audit MUST fail on a missing cell rather than filling it with zero.

This coefficient/degree grid makes field queries and integral torsion cross-checks real release behaviours instead of hand-picked demos, while the explicit finite boundary keeps the completeness claim honest. Coefficient systems and reduced conventions are required parameters in the planned invariant definition. [Domain model: Invariant definition](../planning/01-domain-model.md#invariant-definition)

### Adversarial coverage audit

G5 MUST exercise the states in both stored data and query fixtures without weakening or replacing any G4 exact-grid cell. In particular:

- **exact zero** is an exact assertion with a canonical zero group;
- **unknown** says the mathematical value is explicitly unknown within a stated scope;
- **not computed** says no qualifying computation has completed;
- **absent** says nothing and is never synthesized into one of the preceding states;
- **conflicting** links at least two incompatible retained assertions and does not silently select one as exact;
- a **correction** creates a new assertion with `supersedes`, reason, actor, and timestamp while leaving the old assertion addressable; and
- “forbidden” pattern constraints pass only when exact complete data prove absence. Unknown, absent, incomplete, or conflicting data cannot prove a forbidden condition.

Explicit knowledge states and append-only assertions are foundational domain rules, not display conveniences. [CONTEXT language](../../CONTEXT.md) [Domain model: Result assertion](../planning/01-domain-model.md#result-assertion)

## G6: required query behaviours

Each behaviour MUST have a checked-in normalized request, expected result IDs/order, expected envelope, negative cases, and snapshot ID. Default result ordering is immutable public ID ascending unless a request names another documented stable order; ties always end in public ID ascending. Cursor tokens bind to the normalized query and snapshot, so page two cannot drift after publication.

| ID | Behaviour and pass condition |
|---|---|
| **Q1 Resolve identity** | Resolve one immutable public ID, namespaced external ID, or alias; report ambiguous aliases rather than guessing. Resolve 1,000 public IDs in one batch while preserving input correlation, duplicates, and per-item not-found errors. |
| **Q2 Read homology** | Return all selected homology for one conceptual space or model with degree, coefficient system, reduced convention, knowledge state, canonical value when present, assertion ID, and snapshot. |
| **Q3 Exact group/Betti filter** | Find curated spaces with an exact integral group or free rank in a specified degree; exact-zero and missing/unknown remain distinct. |
| **Q4 Prime torsion** | Find curated spaces whose exact integral `H_n` has `p`-primary torsion for a specified prime `p`. Invalid/nonprime `p` is rejected. |
| **Q5 Primary summand pattern** | Filter by exact cyclic primary summand `(p, exponent)`, minimum or exact multiplicity, exact p-primary decomposition, or p-primary exponent. The response states which meaning was used; “order divisible by `p^e`” is not conflated with “contains `Z/(p^e)`.” |
| **Q6 Structured partial pattern** | Apply required, forbidden, exact-group, Betti, torsion, and knowledge-state constraints across an explicit degree window and coefficient system. By default every required constraint must be proven. An opt-in `include_unresolved` mode may return candidates with unknown/incomplete constraints but must separate them from proven matches. |
| **Q7 Exact signature** | Match an exact integral homology signature over a finite degree range. The query declares whether groups outside the range are ignored or required to vanish under a published completeness bound. |
| **Q8 Knowledge-state query** | Filter and facet exact, bound/conjectural, unknown, not computed, not applicable, and conflicting assertions without converting any of them to SQL-null semantics or zero. |
| **Q9 Models and promotion** | List every model asserted for a conceptual space with assertion/equivalence status and evidence; query the model corpus directly; list computed model results not promoted to a conceptual space. |
| **Q10 Provenance/history** | Expand one result to its source/computation run, model input, algorithm, hashes, review events, conflict set, and supersession chain. Retrieve the answer as it appeared in an older snapshot. |
| **Q11 Computation bundle** | Retrieve a versioned model plus ordered chain-complex artifact, coefficient/convention metadata, content hashes, and run recipe sufficient for a downstream program to validate/recompute it. |
| **Q12 Snapshot/bulk** | Select an immutable snapshot, list changes between two snapshots without offset pagination, and stream a complete or filtered release in deterministic order with a terminal digest/count record. |

This is a release-focused consolidation of the seventeen initial workloads already identified in the implementation plan. [First tasks: query contract](../planning/03-first-tasks.md#task-1-define-the-downstream-query-contract)

### Torsion-pattern truth table

For a condition such as “no 2-torsion in `H_3`,” the interface MUST apply this three-valued evidence rule:

| Selected state for integral `H_3` | Group has 2-primary summand | Group has none | Outcome for `forbid p=2` |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| exact and complete | yes | — | proven false |
| exact and complete | — | yes | proven true |
| unknown / not computed / absent | — | — | unresolved, never true |
| bounded / conjectural | any | any | unresolved unless the bound itself logically proves the predicate and that proof rule is versioned |
| conflicting | any | any | unresolved with conflict references |

This rule is the minimum needed to keep an LLM from interpreting incomplete coverage as a vanishing theorem. The long-term design explicitly requires missing data and completeness boundaries to be exposed. [Long-term vision: LLM access questions](../planning/04-long-term-vision.md#llm-access)

## G7: structured LLM-tool envelope

Natural-language interpretation is out of scope, but an LLM tool must be able to consume a bounded, self-describing JSON result without scraping prose. Every Q3–Q8 example response MUST validate against a versioned JSON Schema and contain this semantic envelope (field names may change before implementation only if the same information remains machine-addressable):

```json
{
  "schema_version": "homology-db.example-query/1",
  "release": {
    "version": "0.0.1",
    "snapshot_id": "...",
    "manifest_sha256": "..."
  },
  "query": {
    "normalized": {},
    "sha256": "..."
  },
  "semantics": {
    "subject_tier": "curated",
    "coefficients": {"ring": "Z"},
    "reduced": false,
    "unknown_policy": "exclude_but_report_coverage"
  },
  "coverage": {
    "release_scope": {},
    "matching_subjects_examined": 0,
    "subjects_with_unresolved_required_data": 0,
    "warnings": []
  },
  "results": [
    {
      "subject": {"public_id": "...", "kind": "space", "name": "..."},
      "match": {"satisfied": [], "unresolved": []},
      "homology": [
        {
          "degree": 0,
          "coefficients": {"ring": "Z"},
          "reduced": false,
          "knowledge_state": "exact",
          "value": {},
          "assertion_id": "...",
          "evidence_refs": []
        }
      ],
      "model_refs": [],
      "warnings": []
    }
  ],
  "page": {"limit": 50, "next_cursor": null}
}
```

Hard envelope invariants:

1. The normalized structured query—not the user's prose—is returned verbatim and hashable.
2. Every mathematical value includes theory/coefficient data, degree, reduced convention, knowledge state, and selected assertion ID.
3. Every result lists the constraints it satisfied and any unresolved constraints; no opaque relevance score is sufficient evidence.
4. Coverage describes the release boundary and unresolved candidate count. Zero results therefore means “none proven in this declared snapshot,” not “no such space exists.”
5. Evidence references dereference within the same snapshot to citations, immutable artifacts, computation runs, and review/correction history.
6. Pagination is deterministic and snapshot-bound. The response limit defaults to 50, consistent with the pinned LMFDB search convention, but clients may request a documented bounded limit. [Pinned LMFDB style guide: browse and search](../upstream/lmfdb/StyleGuide.md#browse-and-search)
7. Validation failure is an error response, never a partial success with silently dropped constraints.

The project brief requires explicit mathematical constraints, explanations, unknown fields, and supporting assertions/models rather than embedding-only similarity. [Project brief: LLM example retrieval](../planning/00-project-brief.md#llm-example-retrieval)

## G8: provenance, selection, conflict, and correction guarantees

The following invariants MUST be enforced by schema constraints plus end-to-end tests:

- Homology assertions are immutable. Editorial correction appends an assertion and a review/supersession event; it never rewrites the old claim.
- A selected/current projection is rebuildable from assertion history by a versioned deterministic policy. It points to the selected assertion and is never a second source of truth.
- Every assertion states subject, theory, coefficient system, reduced convention, degree, knowledge state, derivation type, and evidence.
- A computation-derived exact assertion links to exact input/model hashes, algorithm and implementation versions, parameters, output hash, exit state, and retained log/manifest.
- A literature-derived exact assertion links to a structured reference with a stable identifier where available and a page/theorem/table pinpoint when the source provides one.
- Every model and import retains original source identity, retrieval/version, original hash, normalization/importer version, and contributor/upstream attribution.
- Every `model_of` promotion is itself a reviewed relationship assertion at a named equivalence level. Homology is promoted only if that equivalence preserves it.
- Unresolved incompatible assertions produce a visible conflict set and an unresolved current state. A priority rule may order evidence for review but may not manufacture consensus.
- Snapshot membership is immutable. A later correction changes a later snapshot only; Q10 against the earlier snapshot returns the earlier assertion and its then-current status.
- Source, completeness, and reliability metadata are queryable records, reflecting the pinned LMFDB product convention rather than hidden prose. [Pinned LMFDB style guide: Learn more](../upstream/lmfdb/StyleGuide.md#learn-more-about-box)

These guarantees implement the planned append-only assertion/current-projection split and explicit promotion rule. [Domain model: first relational schema](../planning/01-domain-model.md#first-relational-schema) [Domain model: promotion rule](../planning/01-domain-model.md#promotion-rule)

The release test suite MUST contain at least one full trace for each of: literature assertion, owned computation assertion, imported assertion, promoted model result, unpromoted model result, correction, supersession, and unresolved conflict.

## G9: performance, batch, pagination, and export budgets

### Reference host

The `0.0.1` local performance gate is tied to the machine available during this investigation, recorded on 2026-07-11 with `system_profiler SPHardwareDataType SPSoftwareDataType` and `df -h .`:

- MacBook Air (`MacBookAir10,1`)
- Apple M1, 8 CPU cores (4 performance + 4 efficiency)
- 16 GiB unified memory
- internal SSD with at least 100 GiB free during the run
- macOS 15.5 (`24F74`)

The report MUST additionally record database/runtime versions, configuration, git commit, dataset seed/hash, process memory, cache protocol, and whether the machine was on AC power. Serial numbers and device identifiers are deliberately excluded.

### Deterministic scale tier

Performance is gated on a checked-in generator/manifest for nonmathematical synthetic data with at least:

- **100,000 subjects**;
- **500,000 models**;
- **1,000,000 selected/current homology groups** across multiple degrees and knowledge states;
- **2,000,000 primary torsion-summand rows** with both common and rare primes;
- **3,000,000 assertion-history rows**, including corrections and conflicts; and
- two snapshots differing by at least **10,000** assertions.

Synthetic records MUST be visibly marked and impossible to include in a mathematical release snapshot. This tier exists because the real `0.0.1` corpus is too small to expose table scans and poor pagination plans. The implementation plan already calls for deterministic scale tiers, captured environment, and `EXPLAIN` plans. [First tasks: benchmark harness](../planning/03-first-tasks.md#task-5-add-a-synthetic-scale-and-benchmark-harness)

### Measurement protocol and budgets

Run the database and read service locally in release mode. After 10 unmeasured warm-up calls per case, collect 200 sequential single-client samples (30 for full export/rebuild), report p50/p95/max, and gate on p95. Requests use a 50-result page with compact evidence references unless stated otherwise. Do not include process/database startup in warm latency; record cold-start and cold-cache results separately as non-gating evidence.

| Operation on the synthetic tier | Hard limit |
|---|---:|
| Resolve one public ID, warm | p95 **<= 50 ms** |
| Fetch one subject's at-most-50-degree current homology bundle | p95 **<= 100 ms** |
| Resolve a batch of 1,000 public IDs | p95 **<= 500 ms** and one response preserves all input correlations |
| Q4/Q5 torsion search, first 50, for both a common and rare prime | p95 **<= 250 ms** each |
| Q6 structured pattern over 8 degrees with 6 mixed constraints, first 50 | p95 **<= 750 ms** |
| Next cursor page of the same Q4–Q6 query | p95 no worse than **1.5x** its first-page p95 and within the absolute limit above |
| Expand one assertion's provenance and correction chain of up to 20 events | p95 **<= 150 ms** |
| Diff snapshots with 10,000 changed assertions, first 1,000 changes | p95 **<= 1,000 ms** |
| Stream deterministic bulk export | first byte **<= 1 s**, sustained payload **>= 20 MiB/s**, and additional service RSS **<= 512 MiB** |
| Rebuild current projection from 3,000,000 assertions | **<= 10 min** elapsed and byte-identical selected-ID digest on two runs |

Every benchmark case MUST save its normalized request, response count/digest, timing samples, and relevant database query plan. A fast wrong/empty result fails the fixture before its timing is considered. Offset pagination does not satisfy Q12 regardless of small-corpus speed; the first tasks explicitly require streaming and cursor/keyset access. [First tasks: read API](../planning/03-first-tasks.md#task-6-specify-the-read-api-from-the-query-contract)

## G10: owned computation boundary

`0.0.1` MUST own the high-level mathematical path from a supported finite model to the canonical homology assertion:

```text
versioned finite model
  -> owned parser and semantic validator
  -> ordered based chain complex + boundary matrices
  -> d^2 = 0 and dimension checks
  -> owned homology workflow over Z (and F_p where published)
  -> canonical group/vector-space encoding
  -> versioned assertion + computation-run manifest
```

“Owned” means the repository specifies and tests the mathematical input, output, conventions, state transitions, and failure modes without referring to a Sage/GAP/OSCAR runtime class. Mature arbitrary-precision, sparse-matrix, Smith-normal-form, and database libraries may sit behind narrow replaceable interfaces; reimplementing those primitives is not a release requirement. This is the boundary in the reimplementation doctrine. [Reimplementation doctrine: what the project should own](../planning/07-reimplementation-doctrine.md#what-the-project-should-own) [Reimplementation doctrine: what the project need not own](../planning/07-reimplementation-doctrine.md#what-the-project-need-not-own)

Hard computation evidence:

1. Every G2-counted model completes the owned parse → chain → validation → integral-homology path for all degrees through its finite dimension.
2. Re-running the same model, algorithm version, and parameters emits byte-identical canonical mathematical output and stable input/output hashes.
3. All published computations preserve ordered cell bases and boundary matrices as versioned artifacts. Engine-native serialization may be retained as noncanonical evidence only.
4. External engines never write canonical tables or promote model results. Their adapters emit versioned interchange plus run manifests.
5. At least **50** models—including every G3 torsion feature class, empty/disconnected cases, and both representation formats—are checked against an independent implementation or a pinpointed literature computation. Disagreement becomes a retained conflict, not a release-script overwrite.
6. Property/invariant tests cover `d^2 = 0`, canonical finitely generated abelian-group normalization, Euler–Poincare where hypotheses are met, and agreement between integral output reduced mod `p` and independently computed field dimensions where the universal coefficient hypotheses used by the test are explicit.
7. Failure, timeout, unsupported-model, and incomplete-computation outcomes serialize as queryable states and never become zero groups.

Sage remains a plausible bootstrap/oracle, but its runtime objects must not define canonical storage and it must run outside latency-sensitive reads. [Compute strategy: working hypothesis](../planning/05-compute-platform-strategy.md#working-hypothesis) Representatives, induced maps, filtered complexes, and Steenrod structures remain valuable future owned capabilities but are not required to claim this database release.

## G11: reproducible, versioned release evidence

The release candidate MUST provide all of the following at tag `v0.0.1`:

1. **Version identity:** `0.0.1` in package/service metadata, API release metadata, schema identifiers, and `CHANGELOG.md`.
2. **Test record:** a dated, append-only `TESTLOG.md` entry referenced from the changelog, listing exact commands, commit, environment, pass/fail counts, durations, and artifact paths.
3. **Release manifest:** git commit/tag, schema versions, snapshot ID, corpus-manifest hash, source-artifact hashes, importer/algorithm versions, exact G1–G5 audit counts and qualifying IDs, row counts, benchmark-report hash, export hashes, and known limitations.
4. **Clean build:** one documented noninteractive command from a clean checkout creates an isolated environment/database, verifies immutable inputs, loads/recomputes the counted corpus, builds the current projection, and runs schema plus corpus audits. It may use a local content cache only when every cached blob is hash-verified and the same manifest can fetch/rebuild it.
5. **Test suites:** schema/negative-fixture tests; property/invariant tests; Q1–Q12 golden contract tests; provenance/correction/snapshot tests; owned-computation differential tests; and benchmark correctness checks all pass.
6. **Deterministic snapshots:** two independent builds from the same commit/manifest produce the same sorted canonical export SHA-256 and selected-assertion digest. Volatile run metadata is either normalized outside the canonical digest or explicitly separated.
7. **Bulk evidence:** the full snapshot export, per-record schemas, terminal record count/digest, and a verifier command are present and usable without the running service.
8. **Query evidence:** an automated transcript executes all Q1–Q12 fixtures through the public interface and records response schema validation plus result digests.
9. **Performance evidence:** the G9 report and query plans pass on the reference host; regressions relative to the release candidate are noted in `TESTLOG.md`.
10. **Repository state:** the release tag names a committed tree; generated release artifacts are either committed or content-addressed in the manifest; the documented verification does not depend on uncommitted local files or terminal history.

Snapshots are immutable citable release views by the repository's domain language. [CONTEXT: Snapshot](../../CONTEXT.md) The project roadmap likewise requires schema validators, round trips, captured query plans, repeatable imports, versioned snapshots, and correction without destruction. ## Explicit `0.0.1` exclusions

The following are not release gates and MUST NOT delay `0.0.1` unless a later ticket explicitly redraws the destination:

- web UI, object pages, browse pages, and CW/simplicial rendering;
- natural-language parsing, prompt-to-query translation, an LLM agent, embeddings, vector search, or opaque semantic similarity;
- Serre, Adams, or any other spectral-sequence page/differential schema or computation;
- spectra, stable stems, cohomology rings/operations, generalized homology, and homotopy groups;
- arbitrary space recognition, automatic proof that two models are homotopy equivalent, or silent duplicate merging;
- general maps, fibrations, local coefficient systems, representative cycles, induced homology maps, and filtered-complex computation beyond metadata needed to avoid foreclosing their later addition;
- user submissions, untrusted compute, editorial accounts/permissions, public hosting, distributed operation, backups, and production security hardening;
- official LMFDB integration or a licensing decision; and
- a claim of formal verification or corpus completeness beyond the manifest's declared families, formats, degrees, and sources.

These exclusions track the active map and long-term scope: the current system is the database substrate, not the future stable-homotopy research operating system itself. [Wayfinder out of scope](../../.scratch/homology-db-v0-0-1/map.md#out-of-scope) [Long-term vision: current implementation scope](../planning/04-long-term-vision.md#current-implementation-scope)

## Stretch goals after the hard gates

These are explicitly **not** substitutes for G1–G11:

- expand beyond 250 curated spaces and 10,000 reconstructible models;
- add more model formats and primes while keeping per-source completeness statements;
- store explicit homology representatives and induced maps through owned semantics;
- sustain the latency gates under 10–50 concurrent clients and publish cold-cache/cold-start budgets;
- support deterministic optional-constraint scoring and diversity-aware example ranking, always with per-constraint explanations;
- generate compact prompt-budget views from the same evidence envelope;
- add the deferred web UI and model renderer;
- qualify more independent computation oracles and publish discrepancy dashboards; and
- begin a separate Wayfinder effort for the minimal shared map/fibration identity data forced by Serre work.

## Why this boundary is coherent

The release is deliberately asymmetric: corpus, truth semantics, exact torsion querying, evidence bundles, and owned ordinary homology are hard; visual polish, natural-language interpretation, and spectral sequences are not. That matches the user's immediate aim—fast example lookup and reusable upstream data—while preserving the project's long-term obligation to share identities, models, assertions, runs, and snapshots across later Serre and Adams modules. [Project brief: confirmed downstream consumers](../planning/00-project-brief.md#confirmed-downstream-consumers) [Long-term vision: design obligations](../planning/04-long-term-vision.md#design-obligations-created-now)

The contract also remains implementation-neutral where evidence has not yet forced a choice: it requires observable read behaviour, deterministic snapshots, an owned mathematical boundary, and measured scale, but it does not freeze PostgreSQL, Sage, Rust, a web framework, or a physical schema. That follows the working agreement to derive abstractions and indexes from concrete fixtures and measured workloads. [Working agreements: method](../planning/06-working-agreements.md#method)
